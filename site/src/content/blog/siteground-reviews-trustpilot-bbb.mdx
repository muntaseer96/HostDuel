SiteGround is one of the highest-rated shared hosts on the review sites — but it's also one of the most expensive at renewal. We pulled its ratings across platforms and added the pricing reality behind the stars.

## SiteGround Ratings at a Glance

| Platform | Rating |
|----------|--------|
| Trustpilot | **4.8 / 5** (~26,100 reviews) |
| G2 | 4.4 / 5 |
| Better Business Bureau | Not accredited |
| HostDuel editorial | 4.1 / 5 |

Unlike budget hosts, SiteGround rates **highly on both Trustpilot and G2** — the satisfaction is real. Sitejabber and other platforms echo the same: people genuinely like the product. The catch isn't quality; it's price.

## What Reviewers Praise

SiteGround posts our **top marks in two categories that matter most**:

- **Support 5.0/5** — fast, knowledgeable 24/7 phone/chat/ticket support. Consistently the standout in reviews.
- **Performance 5.0/5** — Google Cloud infrastructure, strong caching, 10 data-center locations.
- **Developer features** — staging, Git, SSH, 30-day backup retention, AI anti-bot/WAF security.

## What Reviewers Complain About

Almost every complaint is about **cost and limits**, not quality (our value rating: 3.0/5):

- **Renewal is ~6x the intro** ($2.99 → $17.99) — the steepest markup among major shared hosts. See [why renewal prices are higher](/blog/why-hosting-renewal-prices-higher).
- **No free domain** included (most competitors include one).
- **Strict inode limits** can suspend accounts with lots of small files.
- **Entry StartUp plan is limited** — no staging or on-demand backups until GrowBig+; $35 fee for extra migrations.

## The Numbers Reviews Miss

| Metric | SiteGround |
|--------|------------|
| Intro price | [$2.99/mo](/hosting/siteground) |
| Renewal price | $17.99/mo (~6x) |
| Money-back guarantee | 30 days (14 for cloud) |

| Value-for-money (our rating) | 3.0 / 5 |

The pattern: you're paying a premium for genuinely premium support and performance. Whether it's worth it depends on how much downtime and slow support would cost you.

## Verdict: Who SiteGround Is For

**Good fit if:** support quality and performance matter more than price, and you'll budget for the renewal. Excellent for business-critical WordPress sites.

**Look elsewhere if:** you're budget-conscious at renewal or want a free domain. Cheaper-at-renewal options: [Hostinger](/hosting/hostinger) ($10.99) or [Namecheap](/hosting/namecheap) ($4.48). For similar quality with a different price profile, see [best managed WordPress hosting](/blog/best-managed-wordpress-hosting).

## FAQ

### Is SiteGround worth the high renewal price?

If you value top-tier support and Google Cloud performance for a site that matters, yes. For a hobby site, the 6x renewal makes cheaper hosts better value.

### Why is SiteGround so highly rated but so expensive?

The high ratings reflect genuinely excellent support and speed (we rate both 5/5). The expense is the renewal price — quality and cost are both real.

### Is SiteGround BBB accredited?

No, but it rates strongly on Trustpilot (4.8) and G2 (4.4). The main complaints are price and inode limits, not service quality.

### What's the catch with SiteGround's intro price?

It renews at roughly 6x — $2.99 becomes $17.99/mo. Lock in the longest term at the intro rate if you commit.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Strong on both Trustpilot (4.8) and G2 (4.4)** — the satisfaction is genuine.
2. Best-in-class **support (5/5) and performance (5/5)**.
3. The weakness is **value** — a ~6x renewal and strict inode limits.
4. Worth it for business-critical sites; overkill for budget projects.
