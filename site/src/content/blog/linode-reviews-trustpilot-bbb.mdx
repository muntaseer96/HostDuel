Linode (now part of Akamai) is one of the most respected developer clouds — and it carries one of the **strangest review splits in hosting**: a 2.0 on Trustpilot next to a 4.6 on G2. That contradiction is the whole story. Here's what's actually going on.

## Linode Ratings at a Glance

| Platform | Rating |
|----------|--------|
| Trustpilot | **2.0 / 5** (~200 reviews) |
| G2 | **4.6 / 5** |
| Better Business Bureau | Not rated |
| HostDuel editorial | 4.0 / 5 |

A 2.0 Trustpilot and a 4.6 G2 aren't measuring the same thing. **G2 reviewers are developers rating the product** — and they love it. **Trustpilot reviewers are people who hit account verification or cancellation problems** — and they're furious. Both are real; they're just different populations.

## What Reviewers Praise

On the product itself (the G2 side), Linode rates **performance 4.5/5** and **security 4.5/5**:

- **Reliable, predictable flat-rate infrastructure** — no surprise metering.
- **24/7 phone support** — genuinely rare among cloud VPS providers.
- **Akamai edge network integration** and a mature Kubernetes offering (LKE).
- **Clean, well-documented platform** that long-term Linux users trust.

## What Reviewers Complain About

The Trustpilot complaints (the 2.0 side) are remarkably consistent:

- **Aggressive fraud/verification detection** — false positives that lock out legitimate new users.
- **Account cancellation friction** — the single loudest theme.
- **Price increases after the Akamai acquisition**, and some reports of **worse support quality** post-merger.
- **Fewer global locations** than the hyperscalers, and no managed WordPress.

The honest read: **if you're an experienced developer who gets through verification, Linode is excellent.** The 2.0 Trustpilot is mostly the onboarding/cancellation experience, not the running servers.

## Verdict: Who Linode Is For

**Great fit if:** you want reliable, flat-priced VPS infrastructure with actual phone support, you run Linux/Kubernetes, and you're comfortable with unmanaged hosting.

**Look elsewhere if:** you need managed WordPress, hand-holding, or you've been burned by aggressive verification. For a similar developer cloud, see [Vultr](/hosting/vultr) (more regions) or [best DigitalOcean alternatives](/blog/best-digitalocean-alternatives).

## FAQ

### Why does Linode have a 2.0 Trustpilot but 4.6 on G2?

Different reviewers. G2 captures developers rating the actual product (which is strong); Trustpilot captures people who hit account verification or cancellation problems. The servers and the signup experience are rated very differently.

### Is Linode legit?

Yes — it's a long-established cloud (founded 2003) now owned by Akamai, with strong G2 ratings and a solid engineering reputation. The low Trustpilot reflects onboarding friction, not a scam.

### Did Linode get worse after Akamai bought it?

Some reviewers report price increases and slower support post-acquisition. The core infrastructure remains well-regarded; the complaints center on billing and support changes.

### Does Linode offer phone support?

Yes — 24/7 phone support, which is unusual for a developer-focused cloud VPS and a genuine advantage over ticket-only competitors.

## Key Takeaways

1. **2.0 Trustpilot vs 4.6 G2** — onboarding complaints vs developers who love the product.
2. The Trustpilot anger is mostly **verification false-positives and cancellation friction**.
3. Strengths: flat pricing, **rare 24/7 phone support**, Akamai edge, Kubernetes.
4. Best for experienced devs; not for managed WordPress or beginners.
