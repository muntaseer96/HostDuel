## Understanding Web Hosting Pricing

Before you buy, understand how hosting pricing actually works.

### The Promo Price Trap

Every major host uses promotional pricing. That $2.99/month rate requires a 12-36 month commitment paid upfront, and only applies to your first term.

**Example with SiteGround StartUp plan:**

- Year 1: $35.88 total ($2.99 × 12)
- Year 2: $215.88 total ($17.99 × 12)
- Year 3: $215.88 total ($17.99 × 12)
- **3-Year Total: $467.64**

That's an effective rate of $12.99/month over 3 years—not $2.99.

### Hidden Fees to Watch

**Domain Privacy:** Some hosts charge $10-15/year for WHOIS privacy. Others include it free. Always check.

**Backups:** Basic plans often exclude automated backups. You might need a higher tier or pay extra ($2-5/month).

**SSL Certificates:** Free Let's Encrypt SSL should be standard. If a host charges for basic SSL in 2026, run.

**Site Migrations:** Premium migrations can cost $150+. Many hosts offer one free migration with signup.

**Email Hosting:** Some hosts don't include email or charge extra. Verify before assuming.

### The Renewal Reality

We created a [separate guide on hidden hosting fees](/blog/hidden-hosting-fees) if you want the deep dive. The short version: always check renewal pricing and factor it into your decision.

## When to Upgrade Your Hosting

Shared hosting works great for most sites. But there comes a point where you need more.

### Signs You've Outgrown Shared Hosting

1. **Consistent slow load times** (3+ seconds)
2. **Frequent resource limit warnings** from your host
3. **Traffic exceeding 50,000 monthly visitors**
4. **Running complex applications** (membership sites, learning platforms)
5. **Multiple high-traffic sites** on one account

### Your Upgrade Path

**Shared → [VPS Hosting](/category/vps)**

Best for: Growing sites that need more resources but don't require managed services.

Recommendations: [DigitalOcean](/hosting/digitalocean), [Vultr](/hosting/vultr), [Linode](/hosting/linode)

**Shared → [Managed WordPress](/category/managed-wordpress)**

Best for: WordPress sites that need premium performance and hands-off management.

Recommendations: [Kinsta](/hosting/kinsta), [WP Engine](/hosting/wpengine), [Flywheel](/hosting/flywheel)

**Shared → [Cloud Hosting](/category/cloud-iaas)**

Best for: Sites with unpredictable traffic spikes or global audiences.

Recommendations: [Cloudways](/hosting/cloudways), [AWS](/hosting/aws), [Google Cloud](/hosting/google-cloud)

We cover this in detail in our guide: [Shared vs VPS vs Dedicated Hosting](/blog/shared-vs-vps-vs-dedicated-hosting).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which web host is best for beginners?

For complete beginners, we recommend [Bluehost](/hosting/bluehost) or [Hostinger](/hosting/hostinger). Both offer easy setup wizards, one-click WordPress installation, and helpful documentation. Bluehost has the edge for WordPress users due to their official endorsement and WordPress-specific support team.

### What's the cheapest good web hosting?

[Hostinger](/hosting/hostinger) at $1.99/month offers the best balance of price and quality. For long-term value, [Namecheap](/hosting/namecheap) has lower renewal prices ($4.48/month vs $10.99/month). Avoid anything under $1/month—quality suffers significantly.

### Is free web hosting worth it?

Generally, no. Free hosting comes with severe limitations: ads on your site, minimal storage, no custom domain, poor performance, and no support. For anything beyond testing, invest in paid hosting. Even $2-3/month shared hosting is leagues better than free alternatives.

### How much does web hosting actually cost?

Expect to pay $3-10/month for quality shared hosting (promotional rates). Renewal prices typically range from $8-20/month. VPS hosting starts around $5/month for basic plans. Managed WordPress hosting ranges from $15-50/month. Our [full pricing guide](/blog/web-hosting-pricing-guide) breaks this down in detail.

### Can I switch web hosts later?

Absolutely. Most hosts offer free migration assistance, and plugins like All-in-One WP Migration make DIY transfers straightforward. We recommend starting with affordable hosting and upgrading when needed rather than overpaying upfront. Read our [migration guide](/blog/how-to-migrate-website) for step-by-step instructions.

### What's the difference between web hosting and domain names?

Your domain name (like yoursite.com) is your address. Web hosting is the actual house—the server where your website files live. You need both for a working website. Many hosts bundle a free domain for the first year. Learn more in our [complete beginner's guide](/blog/what-is-web-hosting).

### Do I need web hosting for Wix or Squarespace?

No. Website builders like Wix, Squarespace, and Shopify include hosting in their subscription. You only need separate hosting if you're building a self-hosted site (like WordPress.org, custom code, or open-source platforms).
