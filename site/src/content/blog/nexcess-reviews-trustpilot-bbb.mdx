Nexcess is a managed hosting specialist for WooCommerce and Magento stores — the kind of host you pick when uptime and compliance matter more than price. The ratings are solid, but there's one recurring caveat you should know before signing up. Here's what reviewers actually say.

## Nexcess Ratings at a Glance

| Platform | Rating |
|----------|--------|
| Trustpilot | **4.0 / 5** (~2,600 reviews) |
| G2 | 4.3 / 5 |
| Better Business Bureau | **A+** |
| HostDuel editorial | 4.0 / 5 |

A **4.0 Trustpilot, 4.3 G2, and a BBB A+** is a consistent, credible profile for a premium managed host. In our own scoring, Nexcess stands out for **security (a perfect 5.0/5)** and **performance (4.5/5)** — exactly what e-commerce sites need.

## What Reviewers Praise

- **Rock-solid for WooCommerce and Magento** — auto-scaling for traffic spikes.
- **Top-tier security (5.0/5)** with **PCI/HIPAA compliance** available.
- **100% uptime guarantee** and strong performance.
- **BBB A+ accreditation** — a trust signal many hosts lack.

## What Reviewers Complain About

The complaints share one root cause — the **Liquid Web acquisition**:

- **Support quality declined post-acquisition** (some support outsourced), per multiple reviews.
- **Hidden/unexpected charges** reported by some customers (transparency 3.0/5).
- **Premium pricing** that's steep for smaller sites, and **no email hosting included**.
- **Only 3 data-center regions** and **no cPanel**.

The honest summary: **Nexcess is a strong, secure managed host for e-commerce — watch the billing and set support expectations.** The BBB A+ and 100% uptime guarantee are real strengths; the post-acquisition support and surprise-charge reports are the real cautions.

## Verdict: Who Nexcess Is For

**Great fit if:** you run a WooCommerce or Magento store that needs auto-scaling, compliance (PCI/HIPAA), and a 100% uptime guarantee, and you can absorb premium pricing.

**Look elsewhere if:** you're budget-conscious, need email hosting included, want many data-center locations, or you're nervous about post-acquisition support. For managed WordPress alternatives, see [Kinsta/WP Engine vs Cloudways](/blog/kinsta-wpengine-vs-cloudways-worth-the-markup).

## FAQ

### Is Nexcess legit?

Yes — it's an established managed host (founded 2000) with a BBB A+ rating, a 4.0 Trustpilot, and a 4.3 G2. It's a credible premium choice, especially for e-commerce.

### Did Nexcess get worse after Liquid Web bought it?

Multiple reviewers report a decline in support quality (some outsourced) after the acquisition. The infrastructure and security remain strong; the complaints center on support and occasional billing surprises.

### Is Nexcess good for WooCommerce?

Yes — it's one of its core specialties, with auto-scaling for traffic spikes, strong security, and a 100% uptime guarantee. It's built for stores rather than simple blogs.

### Does Nexcess include email hosting?

No — email hosting isn't included, so you'll need a separate email provider. That's common for managed hosts but worth budgeting for.

## Key Takeaways

1. **4.0 Trustpilot, 4.3 G2, BBB A+** — a credible premium managed host.
2. Standouts: **security 5.0/5**, PCI/HIPAA compliance, 100% uptime guarantee.
3. Cautions: **post-Liquid Web support decline** and reported surprise charges.
4. Best for WooCommerce/Magento stores; wrong for budget or email-included needs.
