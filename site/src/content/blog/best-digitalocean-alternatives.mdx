"I need to leave DigitalOcean, looking for better alternatives" is a steady refrain in hosting communities. DigitalOcean is a solid cloud host, but people outgrow it for predictable reasons: **price at scale, thin support on the basic plans, or wanting something more managed** so they're not their own sysadmin.

The right alternative depends entirely on *why* you're leaving. Here's the map.

## First: Why Are You Leaving?

| Reason for leaving | Best direction |
|--------------------|----------------|
| Want it **cheaper** | Hetzner, Vultr |
| Want **managed** (less sysadmin) | Cloudways, a managed-WP host |
| Want **better support** | Kamatera, Cloudways |
| Want **EU data/value** | Hetzner, OVHcloud |
| Want **push-to-deploy PaaS** | Render |

DigitalOcean's own entry price is ~$4/mo, so "cheaper" usually means **Hetzner** (from ~$3.49/mo) — the rest is about support and how much you want to manage.

## The Alternatives, by Need

### Cheaper raw VPS — [Hetzner](/hosting/hetzner) & [Vultr](/hosting/vultr)

- **Hetzner** (~$3.49/mo cloud) — the value king, especially in the EU. More RAM/CPU per dollar than almost anyone. Tradeoff: setup fees on some plans and EU-centric locations.
- **Vultr** (~$2.50/mo) — closest like-for-like to DigitalOcean's developer experience, with High-Frequency plans for snappier single-thread performance and more global locations.

Best for: developers comfortable managing a server who just want more for less.

### Managed VPS (skip the sysadmin) — [Cloudways](/hosting/cloudways)

If "leaving DO" really means "I'm tired of managing a server," **Cloudways** (from ~$14/mo) runs *on top of* DigitalOcean/Vultr/Linode and handles server updates, security, caching, staging, and one-click apps. You keep cloud performance without the command line.

Best for: agencies and site owners who want cloud power, not server chores.

### Better support / enterprise flexibility — [Kamatera](/hosting/kamatera)

**Kamatera** (~$4/mo, configurable) offers a la carte resource scaling and a stronger support reputation than budget clouds, with a global data-center footprint.

Best for: teams that need responsive support and custom configurations.

### EU value — [OVHcloud](/hosting/ovhcloud)

**OVHcloud** (~$4.20/mo) is a large European provider with aggressive pricing and strong DDoS protection. Best for: EU-based projects wanting scale and data residency.

### Push-to-deploy PaaS — [Render](/hosting/render)

If you don't want a server *at all*, **Render** (from ~$7/mo) gives you `git push` deploys, managed databases, and automatic scaling. You trade some control and per-unit cost for zero ops.

Best for: developers who'd rather ship than administer.

## Don't Forget the Migration

Moving off DigitalOcean means moving your data, DNS, and (if applicable) email cleanly:

- Snapshot/backup your droplet before touching anything.
- Point DNS to the new host and verify before tearing down the old one.
- If you host email, see [why email breaks after switching hosts](/blog/email-not-working-after-switching-hosts).
- General steps: our [website migration guide](/blog/how-to-migrate-your-website).

## FAQ

### What's the cheapest DigitalOcean alternative?

Hetzner (from ~$3.49/mo) typically offers the most resources per dollar, especially in the EU. Vultr (~$2.50/mo entry) is the closest match to DigitalOcean's developer experience.

### What's a managed alternative to DigitalOcean?

Cloudways — it runs managed VPS on top of DO/Vultr/Linode, so you get cloud performance without managing the server yourself.

### Is Vultr better than DigitalOcean?

They're very similar. Vultr has more locations and High-Frequency CPU plans; DigitalOcean has a more polished ecosystem and docs. Pick on location and price for your workload.

### I just don't want to manage a server — what should I use?

A PaaS like Render (push-to-deploy) or managed Cloudways. Both remove most server administration.

## Key Takeaways

1. Choose your alternative by **why** you're leaving — price, support, or management.
2. **Cheaper:** Hetzner, Vultr. **Managed:** Cloudways. **Support:** Kamatera. **No ops:** Render.
3. Hetzner wins on raw value; Vultr is the closest DO-like experience.
4. Migrate carefully — snapshot, switch DNS, verify, then tear down.
