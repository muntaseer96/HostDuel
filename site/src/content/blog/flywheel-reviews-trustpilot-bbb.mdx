Flywheel built its reputation as the managed WordPress host **designers and agencies actually enjoy using** — beautiful interface, the free Local dev tool, demo sites for clients. The ratings back that up, with a couple of caveats worth knowing. Here's what reviewers say.

## Flywheel Ratings at a Glance

| Platform | Rating |
|----------|--------|
| Trustpilot | **4.4 / 5** (~350 reviews) |
| G2 | 4.5 / 5 |
| Better Business Bureau | Not rated |
| HostDuel editorial | 4.0 / 5 |

A **4.4 Trustpilot and 4.5 G2** is a strong, consistent signal for a focused managed-WordPress host. The standout in our scoring is **ease of use (4.5/5)** — Flywheel's whole pitch is a pleasant workflow for creatives.

## What Reviewers Praise

- **Beautiful, designer-friendly dashboard** — the most-praised aspect.
- **Free Local development tool** — build WordPress sites offline, then push live.
- **Free demo sites** for client work — a genuine agency perk.
- **Affordable managed entry** (~$15/mo) with a generous **60-day money-back guarantee**.

## What Reviewers Complain About

The complaints reflect what Flywheel **leaves out** and its ownership:

- **Owned by WP Engine since 2019** — some users prefer an independent company.
- **No phone support** (chat/ticket only) and **no Redis caching**.
- **No PCI compliance** (unlike parent WP Engine) and **no dedicated IPs**.
- **Entry storage limited to 5GB**, and **fewer features than Kinsta** at a similar price.

The honest summary: **Flywheel is a delight for designers and freelancers who value workflow over raw features.** The 60-day guarantee is unusually generous; the missing Redis/PCI and the WP Engine ownership are the real trade-offs.

## Verdict: Who Flywheel Is For

**Great fit if:** you're a designer, freelancer, or creative agency who wants a beautiful managed-WordPress workflow, the free Local tool, and client demo sites.

**Look elsewhere if:** you need phone support, Redis caching, PCI compliance, or larger storage. For more features at a similar price, see [Kinsta/WP Engine vs Cloudways](/blog/kinsta-wpengine-vs-cloudways-worth-the-markup) or [best managed WordPress hosting](/blog/best-managed-wordpress-hosting).

## FAQ

### Is Flywheel legit?

Yes — it's a well-regarded managed WordPress host (founded 2012, now owned by WP Engine) with a 4.4 Trustpilot and 4.5 G2, especially popular with designers and agencies.

### Is Flywheel owned by WP Engine?

Yes — WP Engine acquired Flywheel in 2019. It still runs as its own brand with its own interface, but some users prefer an independent host.

### Does Flywheel offer Redis caching?

No — Flywheel doesn't offer Redis caching, which can matter for high-traffic or WooCommerce sites. If you need it, look at hosts that include object caching.

### Is Flywheel good for agencies?

Yes — the free Local dev tool, demo sites, and clean dashboard are built for agency and freelance workflows. It's one of the friendlier managed hosts for client work.
