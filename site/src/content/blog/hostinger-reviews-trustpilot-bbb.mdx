Hostinger is one of the most-reviewed hosts on the internet — over 68,000 Trustpilot ratings — and the headline number (4.7) looks fantastic. But aggregate scores hide the thing that actually costs you money: the renewal. Here's what the ratings really say, and the math reviews leave out.

## Hostinger Ratings at a Glance

| Platform | Rating |
|----------|--------|
| Trustpilot | **4.7 / 5** (~68,400 reviews) |
| G2 | 4.4 / 5 |
| Sitejabber | ~2.7 / 5 (much lower) |
| Better Business Bureau | N/A (not in BBB service area) |
| HostDuel editorial | 3.8 / 5 |

The **4.7 Trustpilot across 68,000+ reviews** is real and reflects a genuinely easy, fast budget host. But the **Sitejabber score sits near 2.7** — a gap worth noticing. Different platforms attract different reviewers, and the lower-rated ones cluster on the same themes: renewals and support.

## What Reviewers Praise

Hostinger earns its strongest marks for **ease of use (4.5/5) and performance (4.5/5)**:

- **Cheap entry pricing** — among the lowest promo rates in the industry.
- **LiteSpeed + fast dashboard** — genuinely quick for a budget shared host.
- **Free unlimited migrations** and 11 global data-center locations.
- **AI website builder** and a clean, beginner-friendly hPanel.

## What Reviewers Complain About

The complaints concentrate on **transparency (we rate it just 3.0/5)** and support:

- **Renewal shock** — the promo (~$2.99) renews around **$10.99/mo, roughly 5–6× higher**. This is the single biggest complaint.
- **No phone support** — chat/ticket only.
- **SSH only on Premium+**, **no Node.js on shared hosting**, and **free email reduced to 2 accounts** (a Dec 2025 change).
- **TTFB can spike to ~1.3s during peak hours**, and some report friction with the abuse/suspension department.

The honest summary: **Hostinger is excellent value in year one and a normal-priced host after that.** Budget for the renewal up front and it's a strong pick; get surprised by it and you'll become one of the lower-star reviews. Run your real multi-year cost in our [hosting calculator](/calculator).

## Verdict: Who Hostinger Is For

**Great fit if:** you're a budget beginner or running a WordPress/static site, you want fast LiteSpeed performance, and you go in with eyes open about the renewal.

**Look elsewhere if:** you need phone support, Node.js on shared hosting, enterprise compliance, or you want flat pricing with no renewal jump — consider [DreamHost](/hosting/dreamhost) (month-to-month) or [Hostinger vs the field in our compare tool](/compare).

## FAQ

### Is Hostinger legit?

Yes — it's a large, established host (founded 2004) with a 4.7 Trustpilot across 68,000+ reviews. The main caution is the renewal price, not legitimacy.

### Why is Hostinger's Sitejabber rating so much lower than Trustpilot?

Different platforms draw different reviewers. Sitejabber's lower score clusters on renewal pricing and support complaints — the same themes Hostinger's critics raise everywhere. Both pictures are partly true.

### How much does Hostinger cost after the promo?

The shared plan renews around $10.99/mo versus the ~$2.99 promo — roughly a 5–6× increase. Locking in the longest term delays the jump but doesn't remove it.

### Is Hostinger good for WordPress?

Yes — LiteSpeed caching, NVMe storage, free migrations and a beginner-friendly panel make it a solid budget WordPress host, as long as you don't need phone support or Node.js.

## Key Takeaways

1. **4.7 Trustpilot / 68k reviews** — real, and reflects an easy, fast budget host.
2. **Sitejabber ~2.7** — the gap is mostly renewals and support.
3. The renewal is **~5–6× the promo** — budget for it before you commit.
4. Best for budget beginners who plan ahead; not for phone support or Node.js on shared.
