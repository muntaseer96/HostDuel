## What Reviewers Praise

- **Free WHOIS privacy** included on every domain — [Namecheap](/hosting/namecheap) and many registrars charge extra or upsell it.
- **Transparent pricing** — no bait-and-switch renewal games on domains; what you see is what you pay.
- **1M+ domains managed**, clean modern dashboard, and genuinely helpful support.
- **Budget-friendly** across a wide range of TLDs.

This is the rare provider where the reviews are overwhelmingly about *delight*, not damage control.

## What Reviewers Complain About

The few complaints reflect what Porkbun **isn't** — a full hosting company:

- **No phone support** (email/ticket only).
- **Hosting is secondary** — Porkbun is a registrar first; its WordPress hosting (via WP Cloud/Automattic) is newer and limited.
- **No VPS/dedicated** and **few server locations**.
- Occasional **verification (Veriff)** friction and some email/spam-filter reports.

## Verdict: Who Porkbun Is For

**Perfect if:** you want a **domain registrar** with free privacy, honest pricing, and no upsells — one of the best choices available, full stop.

**Look elsewhere for hosting if:** you need serious web hosting, VPS, multiple locations, or phone support. Pair Porkbun (domains) with a dedicated host — e.g. [Hostinger](/hosting/hostinger) or [SiteGround](/hosting/siteground) for WordPress, or [DigitalOcean](/hosting/digitalocean)/[Vultr](/hosting/vultr) for apps.

## FAQ

### Is Porkbun a good domain registrar?

Yes — it's one of the best-rated (4.8 Trustpilot), with free WHOIS privacy and transparent pricing. It's a favorite for exactly those reasons.

### Is Porkbun good for hosting?

For domains, excellent. For full web hosting, it's secondary — fine for simple sites, but for anything serious pair Porkbun domains with a dedicated host.

### Does Porkbun charge for WHOIS privacy?

No — free WHOIS privacy is included on every domain, which is a key reason people prefer it over registrars that upsell privacy.

### Porkbun vs Namecheap — which is better for domains?

Both are strong and cheap. Porkbun includes free privacy and rates slightly higher on transparency; Namecheap has a broader product range. For domains alone, Porkbun is hard to beat.
